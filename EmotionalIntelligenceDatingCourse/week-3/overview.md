# Week 3: Building Confidence in Ambiguous Situations

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this week, you will be able to:
- Tolerate uncertainty without rushing to resolve it
- Build self-confidence independent of external validation
- Differentiate between intuition and anxiety/fear
- Take aligned action even when outcomes are unclear

## Daily Breakdown

### Day 1: Understanding Anxiety vs. Intuition
- Exercise: Body mapping of anxiety vs. intuition sensations
- Reflection: When have you mistaken anxiety for intuition (or vice versa)?
- Practice: Pausing to check in before making dating decisions

### Day 2: Self-Validation Practices
- Exercise: Daily self-validation journal (3 things you did well)
- Reflection: Where do you seek validation in dating contexts?
- Action: Create personal affirmations for dating confidence

### Day 3: Taking Action Without Guarantees
- Exercise: Small risk-taking in low-stakes social situations
- Reflection: What's the worst that could really happen?
- Practice: Making plans without needing certainty about the outcome

### Day 4: Managing Dating App Anxiety
- Exercise: Conscious dating app usage (time limits, intention setting)
- Reflection: How do dating apps affect your sense of self-worth?
- Experiment: One day of dating apps with clear intention only

### Day 5: Fear Setting Exercise
- Exercise: Tim Ferriss fear setting adapted for dating fears
- Reflection: What are you actually afraid of, and how likely is it?
- Action: Create prevention and repair plans for top 3 fears

### Day 6: Self-Worth Independent of Outcomes
- Exercise: Values-based self-worth assessment
- Reflection: How would you feel if you never dated again?
- Practice: Engaging in activities purely for enjoyment, not outcomes

### Day 7: Weekly Review & Integration
- Review: How has your relationship with uncertainty changed?
- Integration: Create your personal "uncertainty tolerance" mantra
- Preparation: Preview Week 4 themes on authentic connection

## Weekly Challenge
Take one social action where you genuinely don't know how it will turn out (asking someone for coffee, expressing interest, etc.) and practice staying present with the uncertainty afterward.
