# Week 1: Self-Discovery & Emotional Awareness

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this week, you will be able to:
- Identify your core values and needs in relationships
- Recognize your emotional patterns and triggers
- Develop self-awareness practices for dating situations
- Distinguish between attraction based on connection vs. anxiety/fear

## Daily Breakdown

### Day 1: Understanding Your Relationship History
- Exercise: Relationship Timeline Mapping
- Reflection: What patterns do you notice in past attractions?
- Journal Prompt: When have you felt most certain vs. uncertain in dating?

### Day 2: Core Values Identification
- Exercise: Values Card Sort (adapted for dating context)
- Reflection: Which non-negotiables are you willing to compromise on?
- Action: Create your top 5 relationship values list

### Day 3: Emotional Awareness Fundamentals
- Exercise: Body Scan for Emotional Awareness
- Reflection: Where do you feel anxiety/excitement in your body?
- Practice: 3x daily emotion check-ins

### Day 4: Attachment Styles Exploration
- Reading: Overview of Secure, Anxious, Avoidant, Disorganized styles
- Quiz: Preliminary attachment style assessment
- Reflection: How does your style show up in uncertain situations?

### Day 5: Self-Compassion Practice
- Exercise: Loving-kindness meditation for self-acceptance
- Reflection: How would you advise a friend in your situation?
- Action: Write a compassionate letter to yourself

### Day 6: Integrating Awareness
- Exercise: Trigger identification worksheet
- Reflection: What situations activate your dating anxiety most?
- Planning: Create your personal grounding techniques list

### Day 7: Weekly Review & Integration
- Review: What did you learn about yourself this week?
- Integration: Create one sentence describing your dating intention
- Preparation: Preview Week 2 themes

## Weekly Challenge
Complete one social interaction where you practice noticing your emotions without immediately acting on them. Notice: What arises? Where do you feel it? What urges do you notice?
